Literature Survey

The evolution of gesture-based systems has seen three primary phases. Our research addresses the limitations of sensitivity to noise and the high economic barriers of specialized hardware.

OpenCV Era

Relied on contour detection. Robustness was limited by environmental lighting and background clutter.

MediaPipe Pure

Excellent landmarking but prone to false positives when faces or multiple hands enter the frame.

Hardware Phase

Proprietary sensors (Leap/Kinect) offer depth but require high costs and complex external setup.

Technical Deep Dive

Model & Architecture

Our system utilizes a hybrid approach, combining the real-time detection speed of YOLO with the precision landmarking of MediaPipe.

Hybrid Synergy Visual

Single Regression

YOLO frames detection as a regression problem. It divides the image into an $S \times S$ grid, predicting bounding boxes and class probabilities simultaneously.

MediaPipe Refinement

MediaPipe provides 21-point hand landmarking within the YOLO-cropped Region of Interest (ROI), ensuring pixel-perfect gesture recognition.

Gesture based navigation system

"How can we enable seamless, non-contact interaction in specialized environments to overcome the physical limitations of traditional input devices?"

Problem Statement

Modern digital environments require non-contact interaction (sterile medical settings, touchless kiosks). Traditional inputs are physically limiting and unhygienic. Our goal is to provide a low-cost, webcam-based alternative that maintains robustness in cluttered backgrounds.
